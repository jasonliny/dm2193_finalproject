Platform vs. Publisher

Claims

Platform

  • Platforms should be "neutral" meaning they allow and treat all types of content equally.
  • As a result, moderation should be limited.
  • The platform is not responsible for content posted.

Publisher

  • Publishers have curated content.
  • As a result, an active role is played in choosing what content is should or should not be published.
  • The publisher is responsible for content that is posted.

Motivation

Platform

  • Platforms say they are not liable for what is posted on their platform.
  • They can then forgo moderating posts.

Publisher

  • Publishers are held liable for the content published.
  • They need people to review content and determine if it should be published.

The Problem

  • "Platforms" are attempting to rid themselves of any responsibility over content posted by users.
  • However, once these platforms reach such a large scale, the impact of harmful content is substantially more significant.
  • A lack of moderation benefits their revenue streams, especially for advertisers.
  • Harmful content has been shown to have higher engagement, which is what advertisers want.
  • Platforms are also hypocritial, as they already selectively exercise their role as a publisher.
